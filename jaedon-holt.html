Human Trafficking

Human Rights

Human trafficking represents a $150 billion industry annually, thriving on the exploitation of vulnerable individuals for forced labor, sexual exploitation, and organ trade. Traffickers profit immensely from these activities, with minimal risk. To combat this, concerted efforts are needed to disrupt trafficking networks, prosecute offenders, and address root causes.


Raising awareness and implementing support mechanisms for survivors are also vital steps in dismantling the economic infrastructure that sustains human trafficking. By targeting its profitability, society can work towards ending the exploitation of countless individuals and upholding their fundamental rights and dignity.
