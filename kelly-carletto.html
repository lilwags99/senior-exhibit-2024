Children’s Access to Healthy Food

Human Rights

A growing number of households with children are food insecure. Their ability to access affordable nutritious food for their families on a consistent basis can hinder children’s chance at the future they deserve. It is a basic human right that nutritious food is accessible and should not be a matter of choosing between paying rent or routine medical care to afford basic food that provides the nutrition to their developing bodies.


In recent studies, the overall health of children that reside in food insecure homes is worse than those of their peers in food secure homes. The most shocking detail of this growing crisis is that it affects children within different race communities disproportionately.
